# 🚀 Jeremy Redman: Taskmagic

**Founder:** Jeremy Redman | **Company:** Taskmagic | **Revenue:** $3M/year (sold for seven figures)

---

# 📊 Executive Summary

Jeremy Redman built Taskmagic, a browser-based automation software, as a solo founder with just one employee (his CTO). The business scaled to over 60,000 users, 8,000 paying customers, and $3 million in annual revenue—making the Inc. 500 list with a team of just two people. Jeremy's secret weapon was the "Tentpole Strategy": instead of marketing one product traditionally, he built multiple interconnected mini-products that served his core customer base and naturally funneled users to his main revenue engine. After hitting this peak, he sold the business for a life-changing sum in the mid-upper seven figures before turning 38. His story challenges the narrative that SaaS is dead in the AI era—instead, he argues that AI makes the tentpole approach even more powerful.

# 🚀 The Product & Value Prop

---

## What Was Built

> 🏗️ **Taskmagic** — A browser-based automation tool that allows users to click, automate, and scrape messy human behavior within web browsers. Built to solve the limitations of Zapier and other no-code tools that were restricted by API capabilities.

---

## The Gap

> 💡 **Browser automation beyond APIs** — While Zapier excelled at API-based automation, users struggled with tasks that required mimicking human browser behavior—clicking buttons, filling forms, extracting data from dynamic pages. Taskmagic filled this gap by enabling true browser automation.

---

## Unfair Advantage

> 🏆 **The Tentpole Ecosystem** — Jeremy built an interconnected product ecosystem where each product served a specific customer need and naturally upgraded to the next. This created SEO advantages (each product ranked on its own), multiple revenue streams, and a self-sustaining growth engine that didn't rely on traditional marketing.

# 🎯 Distribution Playbook

---

## First 100 Users

> 🎣 **Organic SEO through specificity** — Built Mail Lead as a simple cold email tool. The more specific the product (e.g., cold outreach for automation users), the faster it ranks on SEO compared to general tools.

---

## The Tentpole Strategy

> 📈 **Step 1: Identify customer's next problem** — Taskmagic's customers were business owners, agencies, freelancers who needed sales automation.
> 📈 **Step 2: Build something simple** — Created Mail Lead, a simple email outbound platform. Each product is its own entity with its own SEO lift.
> 📈 **Step 3: Natural upgrade path** — When Mail Lead users hit limits, they click "automation" and seamlessly transition to Taskmagic.
> 📈 **Step 4: Stack ecosystem** — Built LeadQuest (AI lead finder) → Mail Lead → Taskmagic, each feeding the next.
> 📊 **Result:** Mail Lead brought in almost seven figures alone. Taskmagic generated more revenue from Mail Lead users than Mail Lead itself.

# 🧠 Frameworks & Lessons

---

## Mental Models

> 🧩 **Tentpole Strategy** — Build multiple cheap products that serve your core, each ranking on its own and making money, all funneling to the main "temple" product.

> 🧩 **Sell functionality, not information** — With modern no-code tools, you can sell actual functionality, not just info products. Use this to create real value.

> 🧩 **Stack as ecosystem** — Products aren't side hustles; they're serving the same ecosystem. Each product helps the others grow.

> 🧩 **Exit timing** — Sell when you're at a peak (Inc. 500), before you turn 38, when you have a young daughter and $9K/month mortgage. Financial pressure can forceExit readiness.

> 🧩 **Focus on problems** — Share your bad days, not just wins. The toxic positivity online hides the struggles that surprise people into quitting.

---

## The Gold

> ✨ **"Focus on the problems. Have your bad day. Have your bad video. Share it."**

> ✨ **"With today's no code tools now you can sell functionality not information."**

> ✨ **"If you do what I do, you'll get there. It might be product five, but cumulatively you'll hit that seven figure millionaire."**
