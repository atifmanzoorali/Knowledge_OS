# 📝 Full Context

This episode covers Bill Gurley's book "Running Down a Dream: How to Succeed and Thrive in a Career You Love" through the lens of Sam Hinky's journey from aspiring sports executive to NBA General Manager to VC founder. David Senra analyzes how Sam used principles from the book to build his career in sports analytics. The episode also explores three legendary figures profiled in Gurley's book: Bobby Knight (basketball coach), Bob Dylan (musician), and Danny Meyer (restaurant entrepreneur). Sam Hinky went from being the first University of Oklahoma student hired by Bane Capital to becoming GM of the Philadelphia 76ers, and eventually launched VC firm 87 Capital in 2020.

# 💭 Key Lessons from the Book

### 🎯 Immense Passion is Non-Negotiable
> You cannot fake passion. Someone else that really loves that job will outrun you. Someone else has a deep passion for whatever career path you're going down and they're going to smoke you if you don't have it yourself. Bobby Knight said: "The key is not the will to win. Everybody has that. It is the will to prepare to win that is important."

### 🎯 Hone Your Craft Obsessively
> Strive to know more than anyone else about your particular craft. Danny Meyer spent nearly 2 years doing "the best work ever as a student" - studying restaurants obsessively before opening his first place. Information is freely available, so you have zero excuse for not being the most knowledgeable person in any subject you want.

### 🎯 Develop Mentors in Your Field
> Take every chance to meet people known for success in your field. Bob Dylan hitchhiked 1,200 miles from Minneapolis to New York City with $10 and a guitar just to meet Woody Guthrie. When you meet mentors, treat them with respect, ask questions, try to get them interested in your development.

### 🎯 Embrace Peer Relationships
> Connect with people who have the most overlap with you - those on the same journey. Share best practices generously. It's not a zero-sum game. Celebrate your peers' accomplishments as if they were your own. Bobby Knight developed relationships with a swimming coach and gained knowledge he used to coach basketball.

### 🎯 Always Be Gracious and Pay It Forward
> Give the majority of the credit to mentors and peers that helped you. Send letters, send gifts. Become the mentor for others that are coming up the ladder. Bobby Knight learned this from Pete Newell, then mentored Coach K, who later asked Bobby to induct him into the Hall of Fame.

# 🚀 Sam Hinky's Journey

## Background
Sam was born in the Netherlands but grew up in Marlo, Oklahoma (population ~5,000). His father worked for an oil and gas company. Sam was his high school's valedictorian and loved numbers and math from an early age, fascinated by exponential growth.

## Path to Sports Analytics
- **Bane Capital:** First University of Oklahoma student hired. Worked rigorous analytical hours.
- **The Lunch Moment:** At 18 months in, at lunch with mentors, he said "sports GM" - first time he verbalized his dream. They laughed.
- **Moneyball:** Read the book in two days. Decided to quit Bane and pursue sports analytics.
- **Stanford GSB:** Took first sports management class, met Billy Beane, spent time with Michael Lewis
- **Road Show:** Spring break = visiting NFL teams on Southwest Airlines instead of beach trips

## Houston Rockets
- Hired as special assistant to GM at age 27 after 2-hour conversation with owner Les Alexander
- Built software tool for draft evaluation
- Promoted to VP, youngest in the league
- Built best basketball analytics department with Daryl Murray

## Philadelphia 76ers
- Hired as GM in 2013 at age 35 (almost exactly 10 years from when he told his parents)
- Left after a complicated tenure

## 87 Capital
- Launched in 2020 after teaching at Stanford
- VC firm named after LBJ's 87-vote Senate victory margin
- "This is actually his true dream job" - surrounded by curious, smart people
