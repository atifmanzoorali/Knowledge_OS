# 📝 Full Context

David Ghiyam is a spiritual teacher and entrepreneur who teaches Kabbalah wisdom through the DVG podcast. In this teaching, he explores the concept of time from a Kabbalistic perspective, explaining that time doesn't actually exist—and how understanding this can help us "collapse time" to become our future selves now. The central theme is the distinction between body consciousness (connected to death) and soul consciousness (connected to eternal life). Ghiyam teaches that we are tested with instant gratification, and those who learn to prioritize soul over body can access miraculous outcomes, including potential physical immortality.

---

# 💎 Core Wisdom

## The Central Insight

> 🧠 **"If something is going to happen in the future, it means that energy exists right now. If you're going to be a millionaire in a year, you're a millionaire right now. You can collapse that time and experience it in the next hour."**

The sages teach that time is an illusion. What we perceive as "future" is already present in the energetic realm. The delay we experience is our own consciousness—we create the gap between where we are and where we want to be. When we align with soul consciousness rather than body consciousness, we collapse temporal distance and manifest our desired reality immediately.

---

## Key Principles

### 🎯 Body Consciousness = Death

> "The body is ultimately connected to the energy of death. And if it's connected to death in the future, it means it's connected to death now because time cabalistically does not exist."

Every time you lose control, obsess over someone, seek validation, or act from neediness—you're choosing body consciousness. This creates "death" in your life: loss of relationships, energy, certainty, and opportunities. The body will die; the soul is immortal. When you feed the body over the soul, you experience death in various forms.

---

### 🎯 The Test of Instant Gratification

> "When our soul came to this world, it was faced with a test. For whatever reason, we are attracted to instant gratification. We are attracted to things that might feel good in the moment but hurt us later."

This is the fundamental human challenge: we gravitate toward immediate pleasure even when we know it'll harm us later—whether it's unhealthy food, doom-scrolling social media, or pursuing unavailable romantic partners. Overcoming this test is how we "earn" the light of the creator and expand our vessel to hold more blessings.

---

### 🎯 Choose the Uncomfortable

> "If you choose the uncomfortable, you will eventually be comfortable. If you choose the comfortable, you will eventually be uncomfortable."

Your comfort zone is body consciousness. Growth requires doing the opposite of what your ego wants. If you're naturally a fixer who can't sit with discomfort—pause and sit in the pain. If you tend to avoid problems—become more present. The soul always wants to give; the body always wants to receive.

# 🛠️ Practical Application

## Daily Practices

- ✅ **Morning Soul Check:** Each morning, ask "What does my soul want?" not "What does my body want?"
- ✅ **Observe Your Triggers:** Notice when you feel the urge to text someone out of loneliness, check social media for validation, or seek approval—these are body consciousness moments
- ✅ **The 10:30 Test:** Question late-night "hey you up?" texts—are they coming from soul (giving) or body (needy)?

## Reflection Questions

> 🤔 **Where in my life am I giving my power to someone else?**

> 🤔 **What would the creator want me to do in this moment?**

> 🤔 **Am I choosing comfort or growth?**
