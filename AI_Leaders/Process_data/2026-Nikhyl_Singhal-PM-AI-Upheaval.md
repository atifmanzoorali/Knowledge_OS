# 📝 Full Context

Nikhyl Singhal is one of the most connected people in product management — a longtime exec at Meta and Google, CPO at Credit Karma, four-time founder, and leader of the Skip community (the premier network for heads of product and CPOs). In this interview with Lenny Rachitsky, he delivers a sobering message: the PM role is being fundamentally rewritten by AI. The "information mover" PM — someone who spends their day moving data between executives — is becoming a dinosaur. In the next 12-24 months, companies will shed 30,000 and hire 8,000, but those 8,000 will all be "AI first" builders. The key divide: **builders vs. information movers**. Builders (PMs who write code, ship products, use agentic tools) are having the time of their lives. Everyone else is in trouble. Singhal's core advice: find joy in building, or the industry will move past you.

---

# 🏛️ Their AI Thesis

> 🏛️ **The PM extinction is not coming — it's already here.** The old PM role (information mover, stakeholder manager, docs-forward) is a dinosaur. The new PM is a builder who uses AI agents to ship, tests relentlessly, and focuses purely on judgment. If you don't love building stuff, you're in trouble. The skills that got you here won't get you there.

# 💭 Key Mental Models

---

### 🏗️ Model 1: Builders vs. Information Movers

> 🏗️ **The Concept:** The entire PM population is splitting into two buckets. Builders write code, ship products, use Cursor/Claude Code, vibe code. Information movers move decks from one exec to another.

> 🏗️ **The Truth:** The "information mover" is going extinct. Companies are shedding 30,000 and hiring 8,000 — but those 8,000 are all AI-first builders. The job description has changed entirely.

> ⚡ **Maxim:** *"If you don't love building stuff, you're in trouble."*

---

### 🔄 Model 2: The Obsolescence Mindset

> 🔄 **The Concept:** The best engineers (and now PMs) obsolete themselves. You build tools to automate your own job, then move to higher-leverage work.

> 🔄 **The Truth:** AI makes this apply to PMs now. Write an agent to match hires to jobs, to answer community questions, to automate standups. If you can obsolete yourself with an AI agent, there's always better work.

> ⚡ **Maxim:** *"An engineer is someone who obsoletes themselves from everything they do."*

---

### 💡 Model 3: The Joy Threshold

> 💡 **The Concept:** Everyone crosses from fear to joy at a moment — when they first build something with AI and it actually works.

> 💡 **The Truth:** Most PMs are scared. When they finally vibe code something (even silly — a dashboard for their kids, a chief of staff app), they get hooked. That's the moment they cross the threshold.

> ⚡ **Maxim:** *"Joy is the biggest antidote to burnout."*

---

### 🎯 Model 4: The Equal Disappointment Algorithm

> 🎯 **The Concept:** In your "power years" (30s-40s), you have 20 hours of demand and 8 hours to give. You equally disappoint everyone — kids, partner, parents, work.

> 🎯 **The Truth:** Now you're told to also reinvent yourself. This is psychologically brutal. But the one who reinvents crosses the chasm wins. The fix: accept you won't please everyone, and prioritize your reinvention anyway.

> ⚡ **Maxim:** *"The next 2 years requires a lot of fire in the belly."*

---

### 🧠 Model 5: The Judgment Primitive

> 🧠 **The Concept:** Everything automatable will be automated. What remains is judgment — deciding what to build, what's good, what fits the product.

> 🧠 **The Truth:** The new PM job is to judge. Not to write specs. Not to manage stakeholders. To have an opinion on what matters and fight for it. The era of "responsibility without authority" is over — now you either build or you're not needed.

> ⚡ **Maxim:** *"My job is to evaluate. I have to think through what success looks like and whether this makes sense for the system."*

# 🚩 Contrarian Views

---

> ⚡ **PMs don't need to learn to code — they need to learn to vibe code.**

The conventional wisdom: PMs should learn to code. Singhal disagrees — you don't need to be an engineer. You just need to be opinionated on what you want and know what good looks like. Tell the AI what you want and it builds it. The barrier to entry is zero.

---

> ⚡ **The logo on your resume doesn't matter anymore — it might hurt you.**

Conventional: Big company brands (Meta, Google) are career gold. Singhal: Wrong. Companies now ask "What tools do you use? What's your judgment? What have you built?" Not "What company did you work at 5 years ago?" Your past brand can now work against you if that company isn't AI-forward.

---

> ⚡ **Design roles are plateauing — not becoming more valuable.**

Conventional wisdom: As products multiply, design becomes more important for differentiation. Singhal: Wrong. Companies are conflating design with "production" not "taste." Pixel generators vs. taste makers — and most companies hired for production, not taste.

# 👁️ Their Blind Spot

---

> 👁️ **Diversity setback:** The pace of AI change is so fast that companies are hiring people who "look and act like themselves" (Bay Area, similar backgrounds). Women in their "power years" (having kids) can't spend nights/weekends on cloud code. Age, gender, ethnic backgrounds are all taking a hit — and no one's talking about it.

---

> 👁️ **The "adults" myth:** The idea that "experienced adults" will always be needed. Singhal believes this is true — but only for those who stay current. The 50-year-old PM who refused to learn AI is not an "adult" worth paying, they're just someone who got left behind. Being "experienced" now means staying current, not just having tenure.
